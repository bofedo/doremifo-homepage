01 — The problem

Audio branding runs on gut feeling.

Sound is the most emotionally direct medium a brand has — and the least rigorously managed. That gap is expensive.

🎯

Briefs that don't land

"Make it feel premium." "More trustworthy." Without a shared reference, every audio brief is a guessing game — and revisions are expensive.

📉

Sound that contradicts your brand

A single notification tone can undermine months of visual brand work. Most teams don't find out until users complain — or churn.

🔄

No way to validate

You can A/B test a headline. You can user-test a colour. Sound? You ship it and hope. There has been no tool for evidence-based audio decisions — until now.

02 — What you get

A common language for sound.

DoReMiFo translates acoustic properties into emotional outcomes — so designers and clients can speak the same language, and decisions have a reference point.

→

Know what a sound actually communicates

Before you ship. Before the client hears it. DoReMiFo maps acoustic properties to the emotional responses they reliably produce — so you can design with intent, not hope.

→

Brief smarter, revise less

Replace vague adjectives with measurable targets. A brief a sound designer can act on — and a client can approve without endless rounds.

→

Validate before you commit

Compare candidates against a perceptual reference library. Know whether your shortlisted sounds land where you intend — before they go into production or on air.

→

Evidence your clients can present upward

Audio decisions are hard to defend in a boardroom. DoReMiFo gives you data — perceptual scores, emotional positioning, benchmarks — that make the case without requiring the room to trust your ear.
